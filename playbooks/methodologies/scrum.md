# Scrum Methodology Playbook

## Use When

Use Scrum when delivery is iterative, backlog-driven, and the team can inspect and adapt every sprint.

## Required Artifacts

- product backlog
- sprint backlog
- sprint goal
- increment
- definition of ready
- definition of done
- retrospective actions

## Ceremonies

- sprint planning
- daily scrum
- backlog refinement
- sprint review
- retrospective

## Control Metrics

- sprint goal confidence
- velocity trend
- burndown or burnup
- carryover rate
- blocked items
- escaped defects
- unplanned work ratio

## Agent Rules

- Treat sprint goal risk as higher priority than individual task completion.
- Flag stories entering sprint without clear acceptance criteria.
- Detect carryover patterns across sprints.
- Separate backlog refinement from scope baseline approval.
- Use QA and BA agents before sprint commitment when requirements are weak.

## Reporting Focus

- sprint progress
- sprint goal risk
- blockers
- scope changes inside sprint
- quality and test readiness
